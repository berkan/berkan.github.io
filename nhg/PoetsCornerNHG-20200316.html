1 Possible Topics

1.1 Goal(s)

Increasing the overall resilience of the neighbourhood by:

  • Identifying the high-risk (elderly and vulnerable) neighbours
  • Identifying vulnerable households and individuals
  • Distributing information and preparedness materials to households
    • How the virus transmits, how to protect yourself & others, key symptoms – see information posters below
    • Self-isolation preparedness checklist & form, compiled from:
      • Community Mitigation Strategy - CDC US (pdf)
      • Stay at home: guidance for people with confirmed or possible coronavirus (COVID-19) infection - GOV.UK
    • Contacts in the neighbourhood
    • Maybe: "contact us if you have difficulty preparing"
  • Establishing a neighbourhood communication network, by one or more ways:
    • a buddy system (as in Protect the isolated and vulnerable during the COVID-19 outbreak - Neighbourhood Watch Network)
    • a telephone tree (as in Community Emergency Plan Template (pdf), and Community Emergency Plan Toolkit (pdf) - GOV.UK)
  • Creating a database of contact numbers and emails, number and ages of children[?], pet information[?], and equipment and skills that might be useful in an emergency, such as medical training (as in Connect Your Block - Burlingame Neighborhood Network)
  • Establishing a rota for checking-up and supporting high-risk and vulnerable neighbours
    • Maybe: "2 households supporting one household" (as in https://www.facebook.com/groups/epcnetwork/permalink/1764334273708464/)

1.2 Discussion: what happens if/when local services have interruptions?

Especially

  • Less frequent rubbish collection
  • Supermarket closures due to staff shortages

Less likely but possible and can be more severe:

  • water
  • electricity cuts

Less severe:

  • recycling collection
